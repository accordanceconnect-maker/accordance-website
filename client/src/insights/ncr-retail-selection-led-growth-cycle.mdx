## The Shift

Retail activity across Delhi NCR, particularly Gurgaon, has accelerated.

Leasing is up.  
Rents are moving.  
New supply is entering.

At a surface level, this looks like expansion.

But the shift is more specific.

NCR retail is moving into a phase where growth is not driven by presence.

It is driven by selection.

---

## What Is Changing

Leasing volumes across NCR have seen a sharp rise, with Gurgaon contributing a dominant share of activity and both malls and high streets absorbing demand.

At the same time:

- Main streets are gaining share alongside malls  
- Rentals in prime corridors are increasing steadily  
- New Grade A supply is entering the market  

This is not creating uniform growth.

It is increasing choice and competition simultaneously.

---

## The Rise of Multiple Winning Formats

Unlike earlier cycles, retail in NCR is no longer mall-led alone.

Three formats are now co-existing:

### 1. Dominant Malls

- Low vacancy (~3% in superior assets)  
- Stable footfall  
- Anchor-driven consumption  

---

### 2. High Streets

- Strong rental growth in key pockets  
- Integrated with residential catchments  
- Faster leasing cycles  

---

### 3. Experience-Led Clusters

- F&B, entertainment, and social destinations  
- Driving footfall beyond shopping  

---

The implication is clear:

Retail success is no longer format-driven.  
It is location and positioning driven.

---

## Demand Is Expanding — But Selectively

Unlike earlier cycles, demand is not just consolidating.

It is expanding — but into specific corridors and formats.

- F&B, fashion, and lifestyle brands are leading leasing activity  
- Domestic and international brands continue to expand footprint  
- Experience-led consumption is increasing  

But this demand is not evenly distributed.

It is:

- Concentrated in proven catchments  
- Selective in new markets  
- Performance-driven  

---

## Supply Is Changing The Game

New mall completions and upcoming projects are increasing inventory.

This creates two parallel outcomes:

- More opportunities for retailers  
- More competition for developers  

Vacancy levels are rising at the headline level due to supply addition, even as superior assets remain tightly occupied.

This divergence is important.

It signals:

Not all supply will perform equally.

---

## What This Means For Retailers

Expansion is no longer about entering the market.

It is about choosing the right layer of the market.

Retailers now have:

- More options  
- More formats  
- More corridors  

But also:

- Higher pricing in prime locations  
- Greater competition in emerging ones  

The advantage lies in:

Entering the right corridor at the right stage.

---

## What This Means For Developers

Leasing alone is no longer enough.

Assets now compete on:

- Tenant mix  
- Experience positioning  
- Catchment integration  

With increasing supply, differentiation becomes critical.

The gap between strong and average assets will widen.

---

## What This Means For Capital

Retail real estate in NCR is no longer a uniform asset class.

It is becoming:

A layered market with differentiated outcomes.

Returns will be driven by:

- Asset positioning  
- Corridor selection  
- Tenant strategy  

Not just leasing activity.

---

## Closing Perspective

NCR retail is not just growing.

It is becoming more defined.

More formats.  
More supply.  
More demand.  

But also:

More differentiation.

In this cycle, success will not come from participation.

It will come from selection.
